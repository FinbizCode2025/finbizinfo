3.1 Revenue Recognition (IFRS 15)
Revenue is recognised when control of goods or services is transferred to the customer, measured at the transaction price.
Key policies:
a) Sale of Goods
•	Revenue recognised at the point in time when control passes (delivery/despatch).
•	Variable consideration (discounts, rebates) adjusted.
b) Rendering of Services
•	Revenue recognised over time, using stage of completion method.
c) Contract Liabilities
Advance payments are recorded as contract liabilities until performance obligations are satisfied.

3.3 Intangible Assets (IAS 38)
Recognised when controlled by the Company and future economic benefits are probable.
•	Amortisation: Straight-line
•	Useful life: [XX years]

3.4 Leases (IFRS 16)
a) Lessee accounting
The Company recognises:
•	A Right-of-use (ROU) asset
•	A Lease liability (PV of future lease payments)
Short-term (<12 months) and low-value leases are expensed.
b) Lessor accounting
Classified as operating or finance leases based on risk and rewards.
________________________________________
3.5 Financial Instruments (IFRS 9)
a) Financial Assets
Classification:
•	Amortised cost
•	FVOCI
•	FVTPL
b) Impairment
Expected credit loss (ECL) model is applied.
c) Financial Liabilities
Measured at amortised cost unless designated at FVTPL.

3.7 Impairment of Non-Financial Assets (IAS 36)
Assets are reviewed annually for impairment indicators.
Recoverable amount = higher of:
•	Value in use
•	Fair value less costs of disposal.
________________________________________
3.8 Income Tax (IAS 12)
•	Current tax: based on taxable profits.
•	Deferred tax: Temporary differences using the liability method.
•	Deferred tax assets recognised only when future taxable profits are probable.
________________________________________
3.9 Employee Benefits (IAS 19)
a) Short-term benefits
Recognised when the employee renders service.
b) Defined Contribution Plan
(company contributes fixed amounts—expense recognised as incurred)
c) Defined Benefit Plan (if applicable)
•	Obligation measured using actuarial valuation (Projected Unit Credit Method).
________________________________________
3.10 Provisions & Contingencies (IAS 37)
A provision is recognised when:
•	A present obligation exists
•	Outflow probable
•	Amount can be estimated reliably.
Contingent liabilities are disclosed, not recognised.

3.11 Cash & Cash Equivalents (IAS 7)
Cash on hand, bank balances, and short-term investments with maturity < 3 months.

3.12 Foreign Currency (IAS 21)
